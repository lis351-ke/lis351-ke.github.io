Alice in Chains

1990s, Grunge, Sludge Metal

Alice in Chains is a Grammy-nominated American rock band formed in Seattle, Washington in 1987. The band's original lineup consisted of vocalist Layne Staley, guitarist Jerry Cantrell, bassist Mike Starr, and drummer Sean Kinney. The band rose to fame in the early 1990s with the release of their second album, "Dirt," which featured hit songs such as "Rooster" and "Would?". Their unique blend of heavy metal, grunge, and acoustic elements helped to establish them as one of the most influential bands of the Seattle grunge scene. Despite their success, Alice in Chains struggled with drug addiction and personal issues, particularly Staley, who tragically passed away in 2002 from a drug overdose. The band went on hiatus for several years, but eventually reunited in 2005 with new vocalist William DuVall. Since then, Alice in Chains has released three more albums, "Black Gives Way to Blue," "The Devil Put Dinosaurs Here," and "Rainier Fog," all of which have received critical acclaim. Their music continues to resonate with fans of rock and metal, with their legacy firmly cemented in the annals of music history.

Microwave

2010s, Post-hardcore

Microwave is an American rock band from Atlanta, Georgia, formed in 2012. The band consists of lead vocalist and guitarist Nathan Hardy, drummer Timothy "Tito" Pittard, bassist Tyler Hill, and guitarist Wesley Swanson. The band's sound has been described as a blend of emo, punk, and alternative rock. They released their debut album, "Stovall," in 2014, which was followed by their second album, "Much Love," in 2016. Both albums received critical acclaim and helped establish Microwave as a rising force in the alternative music scene. In 2018, Microwave signed with the record label Pure Noise Records and released their third album, "Death Is A Warm Blanket," in September of that year. The album showcased a heavier and more aggressive sound compared to their previous work, earning praise from fans and critics alike. Since then, Microwave has toured extensively and played at various music festivals, including Riot Fest, Bonnaroo, and Lollapalooza. Their most recent album, "Last Christmas," was released in November 2021, and features covers of classic holiday songs. Overall, Microwave has established themselves as a dynamic and innovative band, constantly evolving their sound while staying true to their roots.

Arm's Length

2010s, Post-hardcore, Punk

Arm's Length is a Canadian punk rock band from Ottawa, Ontario, formed in 2017. The band consists of lead vocalist and guitarist Matthew Stephen, bassist Alex Leclerc, and drummer Nick Kolomayz. The band's sound is influenced by classic punk rock and pop punk, with catchy hooks and energetic guitar riffs. They released their debut EP, "Everything Nice," in 2018, which showcased their raw and energetic sound. Arm's Length gained recognition in the Canadian punk scene with their high-energy live performances and DIY approach to music-making. In 2019, they signed with the record label Thousand Islands Records and released their debut full-length album, "Sincerity," in November of that year. The album received positive reviews and helped solidify their place in the Canadian punk scene. Since then, Arm's Length has continued to tour extensively and play at various festivals, including Pouzza Fest and Montreal Punk Fest. They released their second full-length album, "Pity Party," in November 2021, which features a more polished sound and explores themes of mental health and self-reflection. Overall, Arm's Length has established themselves as a rising force in the Canadian punk scene, with their high-energy live shows and catchy punk rock anthems. They continue to evolve their sound and push the boundaries of punk rock while staying true to their DIY roots.
